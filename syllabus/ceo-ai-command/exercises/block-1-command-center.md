> Hands-on exercise to create your AI's memory and first skill.

---

## Exercise 1.1: Create Your CLAUDE.md (15 min)

### The Goal

Create a memory file that makes AI actually know you.

### Instructions

Open the CLAUDE.md template and fill in each section:

**Step 1: Who I Am (3 min)**

```markdown

- Background: ____________
```

## Exercise 1.2: Test Your Memory (5 min)

### The Goal

Verify that AI actually uses your context.

### Instructions

**Test 1:** Ask AI to introduce your company

```
Introduce my company as if you're me, talking to a potential investor.
```

*Does it get the tone right? Use your language?*

**Test 2:** Ask AI to draft an email to a direct report

```
Draft an email to [NAME] (my CFO) asking for an update on Q1 budget.
```

*Does it match your email style?*

**Test 3:** Ask AI what you're focused on

```
What are my priorities this week?
```

*Does it know?*

### If Something's Wrong

Tell AI to fix it:

```
Actually, I never use that phrase. I prefer "..."
Add this to my preferences.
```

## Exercise 1.3: Create Your First Skill (10 min)

### The Goal

Create a reusable workflow for meeting prep.

### Instructions

**Step 1:** Create the skill file

```markdown
# Meeting Prep Skill

When I say "prep me for [meeting]", do this:

1. Look up who I'm meeting with
2. Find recent news about them/their company
3. Check my previous notes on them
4. List 3 talking points I should raise
5. Identify 1 thing I should ask them

Output as bullet points, keep it under 1 page.
```

**Step 2:** Add your preferences

What do you always want in meeting prep?

```markdown
## My Preferences
- Always include: ____________
- Never include: ____________
- Format: ____________
```

**Step 3:** Test it

```
Prep me for my meeting with [upcoming real meeting]
```

### Checkpoint

- [ ] Meeting prep output is useful
- [ ] Format matches your preference
- [ ] You can imagine using this weekly
